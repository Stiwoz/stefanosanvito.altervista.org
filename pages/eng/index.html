Introduction

The world of computing is always full of news and let's create everything one can imagine. Every idea that might come to mind, can be made with the help of one or more IT fields.

Once I started using my first computer, I found that there were also games, similar to those for the play-station. Unlike the latter, however, the computer shows and allows you to edit all the files that make up these games giving the chance to extend the experience of the user by becoming a programmer.

I started playing to get inspiration and ideas to include as changes in other video games, especially to Pokemon games (very popular at the time), creating new graphics, new characters and new events, all purely out of curiosity.

In most cases developing games goes along with playing video games, mostly to test them and to gather ideas and inspiration.

Attending an IT school this passion exploded and soon I switched from editing old Pokemon games to creating real modifications (called MODs) for more modern video games like Minecraft.

In the last few years are born the so-called e-sports which, for a gamer, they represent the pinnacle of competitiveness, something like the Olympics for an athlete. Seeing guys from different nations trying their best to learn and speak english to communicate with each other to win a video game is amazing, and then seeing them play live around the world in a world championship is something else entirely.

Alongside with e-sports they began to experiment methods to use videogames to train people like airline pilots, ship commanders, or even heavy good vehicle (HGV) operators. All tasks in which we can not afford mistakes and that should be performed by experienced people, even during their first time.

These simulators can then also be applied to the rehabilitation of disabled people and introduced as an educational method for children with problems such as autism and Down syndrome, to form in them the ability to think for themselves and not have to depend on other people to live their life, or even for people suffering from Parkinson's disease to delay the progression of the disease.

In this essay I will try to relate how video games can become a profession and how they can improve the quality' of life, not only filling our free time, but giving a second chance to those who could not otherwise afford it.
